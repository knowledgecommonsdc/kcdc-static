Reinterpreting Pop Culture Through the Hard Side of Japanese Idol Music

date Saturday, 30 September 2017 time 1 p.m. to 2 p.m.

👤 Homicidol Maniac 🏢 The Third Floor/Slim's Diner

 Past

The incongruity begins with BiS. (Image courtesy of BiS.)

A revolution is sweeping the Japanese musical landscape, a world where the Idol’s traditional taffeta, lace, and high fashion are platforming not sugar-sweet top 40 hits, but society-challenging music ranging from alt-pop to grindcore.

You’ll begin to understand why the hard side of Idol is a fascinating, terrifying, and often hilarious place to explore while meeting some of the very best that it has to offer. Expect to hear genre-smashing creativity and see most incongruous visuals that will challenge everything you thought you knew about J-pop, punk rock, and beyond. Consider your horizons expanded.

Location

The Third Floor/Slim's Diner
4201 Georgia Ave. NW
Washington, DC 20011
Neighborhood: Petworth
Classes will take place on the third floor. Enter through the side door on Ninth Street NW. This location has stairs, but no elevator.

Past event

Instructor

Homicidol Maniac hadn’t ever heard of idols fronting a metal band, but it only took about 10 seconds to fall in love. Now owner/operator/primary-fart-joke-maker of Homicidols.com, his mission is simple: To make enough other fans of the hard side of idol to entice more of these captivating performers to venture to the West.
