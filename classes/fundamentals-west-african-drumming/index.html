Fundamentals of West African Drumming

date Wednesday, 8 May 2019 time 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 Uasuf Gueye 🏢 Hung Tao Choy Mei Leadership Institute

 Past

Djembe by Federico Filipponi is licensed under CC BY-NC-ND 2.0.

The djembe is an hourglass-shaped hand drum that features prominently in the musical traditions of the Manding culture of West Africa. In this high-energy workshop, we’ll get acquainted with the djembe, find out how to position it properly for playing,  learn a series of striking techniques and basic rhythms, and even learn some polyrhythms, which combine multiple simultaneous drumming patterns into a cohesive whole.

We’ll also discover the history of each rhythm and learn how the patterns we’re playing fit into Manding musical traditions, and leave prepared to join any one of D.C.’s community drum circles with confidence.

This class is open to students aged 12 and older.

This class is an Academy of Funk program, presented in partnership with the 2019 Funk Parade — coming up on May 11, 2019. Funk Parade is a one-of-a-kind parade, street fair, and music festival celebrating U Street, D.C., and the spirit of funk. Visit www.funkparade.org to view this year’s lineup of musical artists, discover more community programming, and find out how you can get involved in keeping the funk alive in D.C.

Instructor

Uasuf Gueye is a D.C. native who grew up in a family of West African musicians and oral historians. Since a young age, he has carried forward his family’s cultural traditions by performing a repertoire ranging from 13th-century folk songs to contemporary blues and jazz works on a variety of West African drums and other instruments — some of which he makes himself. He has shared his musical knowledge with students of all ages in venues around the world, including at Boston’s Berklee College of Music. Uasuf has served as an artist-in-residence at Strathmore in North Bethesda, and he presently performs with numerous music and dance ensembles throughout the DMV region.
