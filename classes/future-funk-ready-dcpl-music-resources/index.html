Future Funk Ready: DCPL Music Resources for Funk & Beyond

date Tuesday, 2 May 2017 time 7 p.m. to 8:15 p.m.

👤 Maggie Gilmore 🏢 Northwest One Library

 Past

DCPL provides a whole lot more than just books and card catalogs. (Photo by Matthew and Heather.)

Think libraries aren’t funky? Think again! The DC Public Library offers patrons the opportunity to download free music, attend live performances, and learn about go-go and other aspects of D.C.’s musical heritage.

At this workshop, a librarian will help you explore these possibilities and more by demonstrating how to navigate the library’s website to find publicly available music resources within the library system. You’ll also hear about the library’s physical music collections and music-related artifacts. Please bring your library card; if you don’t have one yet, sign up for a library card before the class.

Funk Parade is a one-of-a-kind parade, street fair, and music festival celebrating U Street, D.C., and the spirit of funk.

Location

Northwest One Library
155 L St. NW
Washington, DC 20001

Instructor

Margaret (Maggie) Gilmore began her DC Public Library career as a volunteer, helping to manage the library’s sheet music collection. Now armed with degrees in music and library science, she focuses on how the library can better support and expand D.C.’s music culture through programs like the DC Punk Archive’s live Library Basement Shows.
