Tiny Tree Adventures: Bonsai Museum Tour & Discussion

date Saturday, 14 April 2018 time 1 p.m. to 3 p.m.

👤 Jim Hughes, Matt Ferner 🏢 The National Bonsai & Penjing Museum

 Past

D.C. is home to one of the finest bonsai museums in the country. (Photo by wnhsl.)

How can a 200-year-old tree thrive in a small pot? At the U.S. National Arboretum’s Bonsai and Penjing Museum, we’ll answer this question and more about trees “planted in a container” — the literal translation of “bonsai.”

You’ll get an overview of bonsai and the aesthetics, development, and maintenance techniques required to keep up this timeless living art form before taking a tour of the museum’s tiny trees and learning how to select and care for bonsai of your own.

Please bring a blanket, a folding chair, or whatever you need to sit comfortably on a grassy surface.

Instructors

Jim Hughes first discovered bonsai in Minnesota, and he started volunteering at the U.S. National Arboretum’s Bonsai and Penjing Museum within days of moving to the D.C. area in 1995. He kept up his volunteering for the next seven years before becoming the museum’s assistant curator and later moving into the curator position. Jim retired in 2008 and now spends his time caring for his garden and private bonsai collection, along with raising money to support the museum and the arboretum through the National Bonsai Foundation and the Friends of the National Arboretum.

Matt Ferner takes his hobbies seriously and is increasingly infatuated with the process of putting trees in tiny pots. He’s given up on trying to get his friends to think that’s cool, and he’s excited to share the wonders of bonsai with some new enthusiasts. Matt knows just enough to get himself in trouble and carries a shovel in his car just in case he spots good material for bonsai on the side of the road.
