Know Thyself for Professionals and Entrepreneurs

date Tuesday, 8 January 2013 time 7 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

👤 Linda Peia 🏢 Above the Bike Shop

 Past

Our daily lives are brimming with choices and beliefs. Some we’re barely aware we have. Some are downright strange. Why do we think that success drives happiness when the opposite is true? Why does signing forms at the top suddenly make us more honest? And why are we so unsuccessful at sticking to the resolutions we make?

Inspired by the work of psychologists and behavioral economists, this interactive course examines the subtleties of human behavior to gain deeper insight into why our decisions do not always make sense. You’ll gain greater self-understanding and learn a few simple tools that will have an immediate impact on your work.

This is a continuation of previous Know Thyself classes. All past and new participants are welcome; the topics covered vary from session to session.

Instructor

Linda Peia is the founding entrepreneur of the Caribbean Chapter at Ashoka, the largest network of leading social entrepreneurs in the world. She has worked with Ashoka for the past six years in Mexico, Brazil, D.C., and currently the Caribbean. An economist by profession, she has always been fascinated by behavioral economics and why our behavior does not always match what standard economics textbooks predict. Linda is a regular contributor to the Ashoka Forbes Entrepreneurs column, where she covers topics at the intersection of behavioral economics and entrepreneurship. She has an MPA in International Development from the Harvard Kennedy School and is the co-author of the “ichoose2b: Happy and Successful” handbook to happiness and success.
