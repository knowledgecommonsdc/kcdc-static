An Introduction to French Comics

date Thursday, 15 May 2014 time 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 RM Rhodes 🏢 Petworth Citizen & Reading Room

 Past

Lucky Luke is one of French-language comics’ most enduring characters. (Art by Luke Atkinson)

Some fantastic material has come out of the French-speaking world, but it remains largely unknown in the United States. This class will highlight the past 150 years of French-language comics, including the works of Rodolphe Töpffer, Hergé, René Goscinny, Moebius, and Lewis Trondheim — some of which are available in English. You’ll depart with a road map for exploring this fascinating comics culture.

Knowledge of French is not required.

This class is presented in partnership with Petworth Citizen & Reading Room.

Location

Petworth Citizen & Reading Room
829 Upshur St. NW
Washington, DC 20011
Neighborhood: Petworth

Past event

Instructor

RM Rhodes has been making comics for several years and reading comics for even longer. He has been to the Angoulême International Comics Festival and thinks Brussels is the best city in the world for people who love comics. He enjoys writing about himself in the third person.
