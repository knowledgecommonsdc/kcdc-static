Create Your Own Masters Program

date Sunday, 2 September 2012 time 2 p.m.

👤 Alan Webb Alan Webb 🏢 National Portrait Gallery atrium

 Past

Recently, a group of DC residents have begun the process of creating our own masters program together.  Even though we are just starting out, we would like to share some of the common tools of the trade we have discovered for creating a DIY higher education program, and invite you to play around with them with us.  Bring your inner nerd and we’ll work together to transform your hidden learning passion into a learning plan you could do go to work on right away (if you want).  To facilitate this process, we will share some of our own learning plans as examples- and those of others who have gone before us.  We’ll also lend a hand to each other finding support and helpful content before we leave.  For more info, visit the openmastersprogram.com.

Location

National Portrait Gallery atrium
F and Eighth streets NW
Washington, DC
Neighborhood: Chinatown

Instructor

Alan Webb is a facilitator and the founder of the Open Master’s. For the past year, Alan has been just down the road in Charlottesville, Va., working at HackCville, an entrepreneurial clubhouse that sits at the crossroads between the university and the community; he focuses on creating the conditions and collisions from which creative projects are born. Alan’s professional path has taken him from the corporate boardroom to small social enterprises, from Tibet to Australia, from carbon analyst to documentary filmmaker.
