#DoItForInstagram

date Saturday, 7 November 2015 time noon to 1:30 p.m.

👤 Eduvirgen Peralta-Cruz, Portia Obeng 🏢 KCDC Pop-Up Learning Space

 Past

@TheBlackBachelorette celebrates with a bride-to-be. (Photo by @jusjackieb.)

Learn how to do it for the Gram and do it well! From Instastalking to posting consistently, we’ll go over how you can build a strong presence on Instagram for your personal brand, business, or employer.

The class is geared toward people who know the basics of Instagram and are looking to make better use of it. No Instagram newbies, please.

You will learn:

  • #DoItForTheGram: An overview of Instagram and some advice on handles, hashtags, who to follow, and consistent posting.
  • Curate (a fancy word for stalking on Instagram): Get comfortable with searching for hashtags related to your brand, and liking and commenting on random people’s pictures. 
  • Get your numbers up: Instagram doesn’t have internal metrics like Facebook or Twitter. We’ll teach you how to create your own. 
  • Make friends: Strategic partnerships with people in your field are key to success.

Instructors

Eduvirgen Peralta-Cruz, co-founder of @TheBlackBachelorette, has worked on communications in both the private and public sectors for nearly 10 years. Her interests in popular culture, leadership, and women’s empowerment drew her to collaborate with Portia Obeng on The Black Bachelorette. She has an affinity for cake and hashtags.

Portia Obeng is co-founder of @TheBlackBachelorette, an Instagram account that started on a whim and acquired 3,000 followers in less than three months. Professionally, Portia has worked on social media and digital marketing for six years. She’s never seen an episode of “House of Cards” (gasp!), and loves celebrating natural beauty, getting passport stamps, and sharing her Ghanaian culture with strangers.
