British Television Appreciation

date Tuesday, 14 May 2013 time 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 Beth Marlowe, Holly J. Morris, Sami Simon 🏢 Above the Bike Shop

 Past

If you’re tired of being jerked around by “Downton Abbey” and have watched every “Sherlock” five times, join us for an introduction to proper British telly — the stuff you won’t see on BBC America or PBS. You’ll learn about the best and worst shows in a variety of genres; where to find said shows; and how to understand what the heck the actors/contestants/hosts/narrator/snooker announcers are talking about. Tea, biscuits and crisps will be served.

Disclaimer: This teachers of this class are NOT British.

Location

Above the Bike Shop

Instructors

Beth Marlowe loves “Doctor Who,” “Downton Abbey,” and any show about a gruff detective solving murders in an idyllic British town. When not watching marathons of aforementioned shows, she is an editor at The Washington Post Express.

Holly J. Morris has been a writer and editor for 18 years, first at U.S. News & World Report, then at The Washington Post Express. She enjoys editing anything that passes before her eyes, drinking Cheerwine, and spreading the good news of British telly.

Sami Simon is an educational media producer at PBS Kids Interactive. She has a masters in educational technology from the Harvard Graduate School of Education and has worked for “Sesame Street,” CloudKid, and WGBH.
