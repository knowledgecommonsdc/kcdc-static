Growing Food; Treasure Hunting in Our Urban World

date Sunday, 19 June 2011 time noon

Kate Clark

 Past

In this two-part class, students will learn about the never ending treasure hunt and nutritional benefits of the bounty of public urban foliage and fruit in Washington DC. We will troll the Shaw neighborhood on bike or foot and will harvest unnoticed herbs, fruit, and greens, and prepare these foods for a family-style meal at the community center and kitchen Bread for the City. Students can attend either one or both parts of the course.

Materials to Bring: buckets, yogurt containers, baskets, etc. A bicycle to ride around the lower NW quadrant of DC

Location

Shaw Neighborhood + Bread for the City


Address will be emailed upon registration.
