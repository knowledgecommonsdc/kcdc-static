Intro to Jazz Dance

date Wednesday, 26 September 2012 time 7 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

👤 Jonezy 🏢 St. Stephen's

 Past

Learn the basics of jazz dance starting with simple warm-ups, before graduating to some sweet moves. The class will finish with a routine incorporating the moves you learned from the class, that will lay the groundwork for a much jazzier life on and off the dance floor. Join longtime jazz dancer and teacher Jonezy for an evening for those wannabe jazzerinas who never got the chance in middle school …

Location

St. Stephen's
1525 Newton St. NW
Washington, DC 20010
Neighborhood: Columbia Heights
Intersection of 16th and Newton Streets

Details

Come wearing clothes you can move in and jazz shoes (if you haven’t got jazz shoes, soft soled shoes will work. If things get desperate, sneakers will do, but won’t be as effective for optimal dance moves.) Bring a water bottle and be ready to dance. The class will take place in the auditorium of St. Stephen’s.

Past event

Instructor

has been dancing for 20 years and has a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Dance and Choreography from Virginia Commonwealth University. Before going to college, she was a competitive dancer and won more than 50 awards in the competition circuit as well as special awards for her performance qualities and technique. As a child, Jonezy has had the opportunity to perform in the Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade and at Disney World’s Magic Kingdom. As an adult, she has had opportunities to perform in the International Festival in Arezzo, Italy and alongside performers like John Legend. Jonezy has been teaching for the past six years and currently teaches at Elite Dance Studio and Supply in Silver Spring, MD. She has choreographed and taught many works, which you could see on her website. She also holds a BS in Creative Advertising, which she utilizes to market herself as a dancer and create video dance.
