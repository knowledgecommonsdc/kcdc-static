Security 201: Encrypt Your Data

date Wednesday, 1 June 2016 time 6:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m.

👤 Jordan McCarthy, Patrick Lucey 🏢 Southeast Neighborhood Library

 Past

Art by Luke Atkinson

In Security 101: Protect Yourself Online, you learned the basics of keeping your online privacy intact. (If you didn’t, that’s OK. You can still take this class.) What about the files that live on your physical device?

In this class, you will learn about ways to encrypt specific files, folders, or your entire hard drive so that only you can access the data. The class will also touch on the advantages and limitations of each approach, leaving you with the knowledge to make an informed decision about the method that best fits your needs.

This is the second class in a three-part series, and the series will conclude with Mobile Security. Laptops are not required, but you may bring one if you’d like.

This class is presented in partnership with DC Public Library.

Instructors

Jordan McCarthy is a technologist with a passion for social justice. He has worked as a system administrator at a think tank and volunteered to teach digital literacy at public computing centers. Jordan has degrees media studies and science, technology, and society from Stanford University.

Patrick Lucey is a new entrepreneur and recently founded his own online security consulting firm, securED Advisors, LLC. He previously worked as a technology policy researcher at a think tank and as a junior staffer on Capitol Hill. Patrick has a master’s degree in public policy from American University.
