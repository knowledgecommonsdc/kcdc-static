Kitchen Ninja Skills: Fruits & Vegetables

date Saturday, 16 March 2019 time 11 a.m. to 1 p.m.

👤 Olga Berman, Washington's Green Grocer 🏢 Geoff's House

 Past

We’ll practice chopping beautiful fruits and vegetables like these. (Photo by Olga Berman.)

To become more proficient in the kitchen, the first step is proper knife skills. In this hands-on class with the creator of the Mango & Tomato food blog, you’ll learn how to properly chop common fruits and vegetables and gain the confidence to apply these techniques at home to ensure your food looks, cooks, and tastes great.

Produce will be provided by Washington’s Green Grocer, and there are a few things you’re encouraged to bring:

  • A cutting board and chef’s knife (it’s best to practice with your own)
  • One fruit or vegetable you’d like to learn learn how to cut
  • Containers or bags if you’d like to take your fresh-cut produce home

You’ll also be asked to sign a waiver; we’ll have copies available at the class.

Instructors

Olga Berman is the creator of the food-centric blog Mango & Tomato and thinks about dinner while still having breakfast. Olga’s recipes are inspired by her childhood in Russia and travels through the U.S. and abroad. Nothing makes her happier than when a reader makes one of her recipes and loves it. When not obsessing about food, she salsa dances, makes jewelry, grows her collection of plants, and plans her next trip.

🌎 mangotomato.com

Washington’s Green Grocer was founded in 1994 to support local farmers and makers by delivering their goods to consumers in D.C., Maryland, Virginia, and Delaware. WGG’s catalog of local and organic produce, farm-fresh dairy, grass-fed meats, and recipe kits all speak to its mission: bettering our local food system and providing access to the wonderful bounty it has to offer!
