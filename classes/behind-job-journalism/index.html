Behind the Job: Journalism

date Thursday, 9 February 2017 time 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 David Colton 🏢 cove Chinatown

 Past

Desk Set by borderhacker is licensed under CC BY-NC 2.0.

What does it mean to be a journalist in an era where news is reported in real time and fake news feels just as prevalent as real news? This class will give you a behind-the-scenes press pass … to journalism itself.

We’ll discuss how the advancement of technology has impacted journalism, how American journalism differs from the rest of the world, and where news is headed next. The remainder of the class will then be Q&A and open discussion, so bring any questions you have about this ever-changing industry!

Location

cove Chinatown
601 I St. NW
Washington, DC 20001
Neighborhood: Chinatown

Past event

Instructor

David Colton is former executive editor at USA TODAY. As a reporter and editor, Colton has covered 13 political conventions and was Page One Editor at USA TODAY during 9/11. He was named an executive editor during the newspaper’s transformation into one of the nation’s top digital news sources.
