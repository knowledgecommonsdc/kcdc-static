The Art of Puppetry

date Saturday, 7 February 2015 time 2:30 p.m. to 4:30 p.m.

👤 Scott Whalen 🏢 The banished? ARTillery

 Past

Can you find the puppet in this photo?

Learn the basics of puppet performance from D.C.’s Pointless Theatre, a company that uses puppets in its genre-bending spectacles. Pointless members will explain puppetry terminology and demonstrate some fundamental techniques of puppet manipulation. You’ll practice with Pointless’ “Hugos” — cardboard puppets controlled by a group — but you’re welcome to bring puppets of your own, if you like.

Instructor

Scott Whalen is a founding company member of Pointless Theatre, where he serves as a core ensemble member and  co-director of communications. With Pointless, Scott has performed in nine original puppet spectacles; among them are “Sleeping Beauty: A Puppet Ballet,” “Minnie the Moocher,” “Canterbury,” and “Hugo Ball: A Super Spectacular Dada Adventure.” He has experience working in a number of different puppetry styles, including shadow, tabletop, hand and rod, and Bunraku.
