Citizen Circles Kickoff

♫ KCDC Event date Saturday, 2 February 2013 time 6 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 Alan Webb 🏢 Above the Bike Shop

 Past

Our January session might be over, but that doesn’t mean you can’t keep learning. We’ll advise you on how to start your own “class,” or Citizen Circle, where everyone contributes to teaching instead of a dedicated instructor.

Citizen Circles are:

  1. 3 or more self-directed learners.
  2. Who come together 6 or more times.
  3. All contributing according to their own abilities.
  4. Using all of the collective experience, resources, & knowledge at their disposal.
  5. In order to explore a particular topic, skill, project, or question further.

Citizen Circles have been organized in DC over the last two years — with the help of Hub DC — on topics like Creative Writing, Strengthening Personal Resilience, Systems Dynamics, and Community Wealth Building.

If you are interested in continuing to explore a topic you began learning at KCDC – or something completely different – join us. We’ll provide a more robust explanation and some timeworn advice about how Citizen Circles work.

Check back in this space for proposed topics, or submit your own to cocreate@citizencircles.com

Instructor

Alan Webb is a facilitator and the founder of the Open Master’s. For the past year, Alan has been just down the road in Charlottesville, Va., working at HackCville, an entrepreneurial clubhouse that sits at the crossroads between the university and the community; he focuses on creating the conditions and collisions from which creative projects are born. Alan’s professional path has taken him from the corporate boardroom to small social enterprises, from Tibet to Australia, from carbon analyst to documentary filmmaker.
