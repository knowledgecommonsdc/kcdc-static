Journalism Gig Acquisition

date Saturday, 25 May 2013 time 1 p.m. to 3 p.m.

👤 Holley Simmons, Holly J. Morris, Rachel Kaufman 🏢 Washington Post

 Past

Learn how to apply for a journalism job from the perspective of a hiring manager and successful applicants, and how Elle Woods’ application to Harvard Law School (in “Legally Blonde”) can get you an audience with key players.

In search of freelance assignments? Rachel Kaufman, author of “Cover Letters of Creative People,” will school you in crafting pitches that will lure editors into your writerly embrace.

We’ll spend about half the class on resumes/cover letters/portfolios and the other half on freelance pitches.

Instructors

Holley Simmons is the lifestyle and dining editor of The Washington Post Express. When she’s not reporting on local fashion, beauty, and restaurants, you can find her sewing a dress from a 1950s pattern or planting a windowsill herb garden. A New Jersey native, she likes porkroll egg and cheese and says the word “drawer” funny.

🌎 holleysimmons.com

Holly J. Morris has been a writer and editor for 18 years, first at U.S. News & World Report, then at The Washington Post Express. She enjoys editing anything that passes before her eyes, drinking Cheerwine, and spreading the good news of British telly.
