How to Shuck an Oyster

date Saturday, 30 June 2018 time 2 p.m. to 4 p.m.

👤 Gardner Douglas 🏢 Hostelling International

 Past

Shucking doesn’t require you to become a champion, but it doesn’t hurt to learn from one. (Photo by Chesapeake Bay Program.)

Oysters show up on fancy restaurant menus all around D.C., but they don’t have to be intimidating! From oyster anatomy and history to shucking and enjoying oysters on your own, this class will answer all of your questions about these beautiful bivalves.

Come hungry and leave with the knowledge necessary to approach the raw bar with confidence — or to set up your own at home! You’ll be working with fresh live oysters, and inexperienced shuckers often get messy, so please dress accordingly.

Please bring $10 in cash to cover the cost of your oysters and shucking supplies. (We waive fees when needed.)

Location

Instructor

Gardner Douglas, aka “Oyster Ninja,” is a champion oyster shucker hailing from Virginia’s Eastern Shore. In his ten years of shucking experience, Gardner has become a sought-after expert working with caterers, bars, and party hosts in the D.C. area. He also owns and operates an oyster catering service and mobile raw bar that travels to high-profile events across the region. You can find Gardner sharing his oyster knowledge with the world via his podcast, “The Oyster Ninja.”
