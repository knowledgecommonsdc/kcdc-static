CANCELLED: Finding Poetry Between the Lines

date Saturday, 28 September 2013 time 2 p.m. to 4 p.m.

👤 Jenni B. Baker 🏢 Washington Post

 Past

We’ll trace the history of found poetry — from its origins in Tristan Tzara’s “How to Make a Dadaist Poem” and William S. Burroughs’ cut-up method to its contemporary incarnations — and review popular techniques for creating a found poem.

You’ll also get the chance to try your hand at creating found poetry through a hands-on activity. We’ll wrap things up with a short reading where you can opt to share what you’ve written.

No previous experience writing poetry is required. The session is appropriate for individuals of any age who have a desire to experiment with language and writing. Please bring a hard-copy section of your favorite newspaper.

Instructor

Jenni B. Baker is the founder and editor-in-chief of The Found Poetry Review. She is the architect of Pulitzer Remix, an initiative that united 85 poets to create more than 2,500 found poems from works that won the Pulitzer Prize in fiction, which took place during National Poetry Month 2013. Her own poetry (found and not) has been published in more than 20 literary journals.
