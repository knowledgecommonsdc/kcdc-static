Behind the Scenes: Good Food Markets

date Saturday, 24 September 2016 time 2 p.m. to 3:30 p.m.

👤 Philip Sambol 🏢 Good Food Markets

 Past

Good Food Markets opened its pilot location at 2006 Rhode Island Ave. NE in January 2015.

Good Food Markets is a mission-driven grocery dedicated to developing retail solutions that work in — and, most importantly, for — food desert communities. GFM focuses on fresh produce and other perishables that have often been missing in low-density, low-income neighborhoods throughout the country.

Philip Sambol, the director of partnerships at GFM, will provide a glimpse into the internal workings of the store, highlighting the structural, sourcing, and community engagement activities that set GFM apart from other retailers in underserved neighborhoods.

Location

Good Food Markets
2006 Rhode Island Ave. NE
Washington, DC
Neighborhood: Brookland

Past event

Instructor

Philip Sambol is the director of partnerships at Good Food Markets. Prior to joining GFM, Philip managed his family’s world-renowned restaurant, Bob’s Steak & Chop House, performed with the rock band The Strange Boys, founded the HECK OF A Neighborhood Grocery in New Orleans, and apprenticed at the Center for Environmental Farming Systems in North Carolina. After overseeing the launch of GFM’s pilot location on Rhode Island Avenue NE in January 2015, Philip helped form a stand-alone nonprofit organization, Oasis Community Partners, to continue the educational, research, advocacy, and community engagement core to the Good Food Markets mission.
