Coffee: Origins, Roasting, Blending, and Brewing

date Sunday, 21 September 2014 time 1 p.m. to 3 p.m.

👤 Carlos Payes, Elizabeth Ryan 🏢 Hostelling International

 Past

Art by Luke Atkinson

Join the founders of Café Los Sueños, a D.C.-based specialty coffee roastery, for an introduction to all things coffee. You’ll learn:

  • Where coffee comes from and how it’s priced;
  • How to brew using a pour-over cone, Chemex, and French press;
  • How different roasts affect flavor.

Before you go, you’ll create your own custom blend using a variety of beans. Please bring $5-6 to cover materials consumed during class. 

Location

Hostelling International
1009 11th St. NW
Washington, DC 20001
Neighborhood: Mount Vernon Square

Past event

Instructors

Carlos Payes is Café Los Sueños’ co-founder and master of the roast. As a lifelong coffee farmer and harvester from the mountains of El Salvador, Carlos has extensive knowledge of coffee cultivation and processing. At Cafe Los Suenos, he oversees the entire roasting process, from selecting the samples and perfecting the profiles to carefully brewing an amazing cup.

email carlos@cafelossuenos.com

Elizabeth Ryan, co-founder of Café Los Sueños, has more ideas than hours in the day, but it serves her well as the head of sales and social responsibility programs. She manages Cafe Los Suenos’ day-to-day operations and outreach activities.

email elizabeth@cafelossuenos.com
