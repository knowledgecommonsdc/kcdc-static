Edit Anything!

date Monday, 2 March 2015 time 6:30 p.m. to 8 p.m.

👤 Holly J. Morris 🏢 Washington Post

 Past

Students edit a box of pasta.

Any piece of writing can be improved upon (great literature is exempt, because great editors got to it first). That writing might be yours, in which case Holly will teach you to self-edit. That writing might be other people’s, in which case she’ll teach you to tear their work to shreds in a tactful and constructive manner. You’ll learn to follow her patented (not really) editing checklist, the same one she uses at work. Hilariously execrable examples that may or may not have made her cry on first read will be employed.

Instructor

Holly J. Morris has been a writer and editor for 18 years, first at U.S. News & World Report, then at The Washington Post Express. She enjoys editing anything that passes before her eyes, drinking Cheerwine, and spreading the good news of British telly.
