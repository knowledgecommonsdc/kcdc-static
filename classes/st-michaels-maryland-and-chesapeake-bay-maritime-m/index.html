St. Michaels, Maryland, and the Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum

date Saturday, 7 February 2015 time 10 a.m. to 5:30 p.m.

👤 David Ramos 🏢 TBD

 Past

Let’s visit the Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum in St. Michaels, Md.!

Ever wanted to see the Chesapeake or to learn about the region’s culture and environment? We’ll drive together out to the waterside town of St. Michaels, Md. (pop. 1,029), explore the town, and visit the Chesapeake Bay Maritime Museum. We’ll stop for local food along the way.

The view out the car window can teach us volumes. We’ll use the drive out to St. Michaels as an opportunity to study the development of American cities, suburbs, and towns, and to think about how wild places and urban areas relate to one another.

You do not need a car to participate, but if you have one and are willing to carpool, please email contact@knowledgecommonsdc.org.

David Ramos is a designer, developer, and design educator. He works on projects that support entrepreneurs, advocates, environmentalists, and journalists. He teaches at American University, offering classes that integrate culture, code, and form. David is interested in landscape history and ways of interpreting the places that surround us.
