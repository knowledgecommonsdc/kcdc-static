Waste Not: Food Waste, Resource Conservation, and You

date Sunday, 23 October 2016 time 5 p.m. to 6:30 p.m.

👤 Will Reid 🏢 Takoma Park NatureLab

 Past

The American food industry discards 150 million pounds of food every day — the equivalent of 523 Titanics per year.

This class will review the causes of food waste and ways to reduce it, debunking myths along the way. (For example, is the food you are throwing out actually bad?) In addition, you’ll learn how to co-opt this system of entitlement to help feed the world and save the environment, and how to limit your participation in systems of economic oppression.

Location

Takoma Park NatureLab
7003 Carroll Ave.
Takoma Park, MD
Neighborhood: Takoma Park
Three blocks from the Takoma Metro station

Past event

Instructor

Will Reid is an advocate against food waste who, in an effort to prove a point, has only spent $5.50 on food for the past two years. He is an organizer for Food Not Bombs DC, an expert in urban foraging, and an advocate of equitable access to healthy food.
